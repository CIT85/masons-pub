What Are Ecosystems?

An ecosystem is a community of living organisms interacting with each other and with their environment. Animals, plants, soil, water, and climate work together to form balanced systems that support life.

Why Ecosystems Matter

  • They provide food, water, and shelter for animals
  • They maintain biodiversity
  • They help regulate climate and air quality
  • They support complex food chains

Types of Ecosystems

Forests

Forests are home to most of the world's terrestrial animals and provide natural carbon storage.

Oceans

Oceans cover over 70% of Earth and support millions of species, from microscopic plankton to whales.

Grasslands

Grasslands support large grazing animals and predators that rely on open habitats.

Freshwater

Rivers, lakes, and wetlands provide essential water sources and support fish, amphibians, and birds.

Threats to Ecosystems

Human activities such as deforestation, pollution, climate change, and habitat destruction are damaging ecosystems worldwide. Protecting them helps preserve biodiversity and the future of wildlife.
