Featured Habitats

  • Forests — Forests are rich environments filled with trees, plants, insects, and mammals. They provide oxygen, regulate climate, and support thousands of species.
  • Grasslands — Wide open areas dominated by grasses. Grasslands support grazing animals like bison and zebras, as well as predators such as lions and cheetahs.
  • Freshwater — Includes rivers, lakes, streams, and wetlands. These habitats support frogs, fish, beavers, insects, and many plant species essential to ecosystems.
  • Marine — Oceans and coral reefs that host fish, whales, dolphins, and countless small organisms. Coral reefs are biodiversity hotspots filled with life.
  • Deserts — Dry regions with extreme temperatures. Animals like lizards, foxes, and camels have special adaptations to survive low water and intense heat.
  • Tundra — Cold, treeless habitats near the Arctic. Animals such as caribou, snowy owls, and arctic foxes survive using thick fur and seasonal behaviors.

Why Habitats Matter

Habitats provide animals with food, water, shelter, and space. Each habitat supports unique species that rely on its climate and resources to survive. Protecting natural habitats helps maintain biodiversity, keeps ecosystems healthy, and ensures wildlife populations can thrive for future generations.
